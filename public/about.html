About Myself

I am a self-taught, self-motivated web developer. I have always had an interest in programming going back over 15 years ago. I received my first certificate degree in software development from North Eastern University in summer of 2001, it encompassed 10 courses over 8 semesters, 6 were programming courses and 4 were computer courses. I tried to seek employment in programming thereafter but was detered due to lack of experience and just wasn't sure it would be the right job for me.
At the time i was working as an independent agent for xerox repairing copiers, it seemed at the time that perhaps this is what i really wanted to do, considering i took this job in October of 1999 after working for 16 years in the food service industry i needed a change in scenery. In 2003 i went to Wentworth Institute going for a bachelors degree in Applied Science in Electronics Engineeringi received my Associates Degree in June 2005, finished up the summer semester but had to leave due to lack of student aid. I have been working as a field engineer since 1999, 3 years ago i was sort of reintroduced to programming by a freind of mine , although i have had some courses on programming in the past, i thought it would be a great idea to rekindle my interest in programming languages. I began taking courses on sites like Udemy.com, Codecademy.com, Udacity, Code School and Edx, just to name a few. I began the freecodecamp's front end developer program in Nov 2015, got my certificate in Feb 2016, then began an onlineMobile Friendly Web Developer program in April 2016 with Capella University throughCapella Education and Careerbuildercompleted the program and recieved ciertificate in June 2016, I have experience in HTML CSS3, and some experience in Javascript/jQuery.
I have always been a technical person and self-taught learner, in my early years i have build multiple computer systems from scratch, i would also sell some of the systems to by parts and buil a better computer, i have done have done this for bout 5 years, put it aside for other hobbies, then picked it back up in early 2004. In my early years i worked as a gas jockey, mason for 2 years, carpenter for 2 years etc.... I have been through various trade jobs, but none of them were interesting enough to remain employed in them. Long story short, i have decided that programming is what i really want, i now have a love for creating web sites.
